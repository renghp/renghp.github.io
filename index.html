About Me

Postdoctoral Researcher in Human-Computer Interaction and Augmented Reality at KTH - Sweden, currently working with data visualization in AR/VR for a pharmaceutical manufacturing scenario, partnered with AstraZeneca. Obtained his Ph.D. degree in Computer Science at RMIT - Australia, working with sonified augmented reality; and his M.Sc. in Computer Science at UFRGS - Brazil, working with situated data visualization. Taught courses in HCI, AI, Mixed Reality, and game and web development, at KTH, RMIT, and UFRGS. Worked in several HCI-related projects in AR/VR, including audio AR interfaces for visually impaired people, at RMIT, situated visualization of electromagnetic fields at Halmstad University - Sweden, and an AR-based platform for the teaching of physics, at UFRGS. Also holds an IT degree from IFRS - Brazil, and a BSc. in Computer Science from UFRGS, with a sandwich period at Radford University and the Illinois Institute of Technology - USA.

Research Projects

SMART Pharmaceutical Industry  

Involving Digital Futures, KTH, and AstraZeneca, the aim is to improve the productivity of packaging lines within the SweOps Steriles function, as measured by Overall Equipment Effectiveness and competence in line staffing. We aim to achieve this by enabling next-best-action decision support for front-line operators.

Main outputs:
📓 Journal paper on Digital Twinning
📖 Lit Review on XR solutions for maintenance
📃 WiP paper on Conversational AI for training
📃 Demo paper on Situated AR for training

Next-Gen Augmented Training Systems 

We explore how audio AR can help users to have accurate and safe performances in manual tasks. By conducting user evaluations of sonified AR in simulated and real-life scenarios, we promote next gen micro-guidance methods for low-visibility scenarios.

Main outputs:
🏆 Best Doctoral Thesis award
🏅 Finalist at Cross-Reality competition
📓 Journal paper on sonified guidance
🏫 Conference paper on micro-guidance sonification

Empathy for the Difference 

In the context of promoting empathy among people without disabilities, we use VR to allow users to embody different conditions. By providing visual and haptic feedback, our applications offer multisensory experiences to enhance the sense of embodiment.

Main outputs:
🏆 3DUI Contest Winner - Empathy for the Difference
📓 Journal paper on embodied visuospatial perspective taking
🏫 Conference paper on embodied empathy
📃 Workshop paper on embodiment of amputation

Situated AR Data Visualization  

We explore the use of data vis and AR to provide information to analyze spatially situated data where it was originally measured. Three use cases were designed, implemented and tested in different task contexts: classroom seat analysis, GPS navigation and EMC data-extraction.

Main outputs:
🏆 Best Poster award
🥈 Award on Data Physicalization
📓 Journal paper on EMC vis
🏫 Conference paper on decision-making
🏫 Conference Paper on GPS navigation SitVis

Virtual Laboratory AVATAR  

By making virtual labs directly usable in teaching activities in Science and Mathematics, we seek to highlight different ways that use technology in the classroom to enrich teaching, allowing students richer resources in interactive multimedia on devices they are already familiar with.

Main outputs:
🥉 Award at the Workshop de Inovacao EaD
📓 Journal paper on AR frameworks for education apps
📓 Journal paper on on biosensors in education
🏫 Conference Paper on attention span in AR
