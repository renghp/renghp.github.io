What exactly is this?

This survey regards the development of a tool that promotes the feeling of empathy towards blind and visually impaired people, as well as an invitation to participate in a longer in-person experiment at another date. Even if you do not desire to physically participate in the longer experiment introduced at the end of the form, we would still very much appreciate your participation in just answering this questionnaire, as having the opinions of a larger population will help us in running our study. The questionnaire is composed of 4 sets of questions, and it takes 5 to 10 minutes to respond. The questions will collect some anonymous information (such as your gender, age and vision condition), as well as ask the way you feel about certain issues the visually impaired community faces.

If at the end of the questionnaire you decide to participate in a longer in-person study, more details will be provided along the form. Summarizing, we are studying how the effects of sonification methods (sound feedback based on user interaction with a system) towards Blind and Visually Impaired (BVI) people may affect the development of Assistive Technologies. Thus, you will be invited to participate in a trial for using sonification methods in navigational tasks.

How is that going to help BVI people?

By understanding the best audio feedback to guide a person in a particular activity, it is possible to optimize the time they take in it, how accurately they can perform it, how confident they feel while doing it, and even minimize the mental and physical effort needed for it. Most BVI people rely on memorizing object locations and refining the search for them by reaching out their arm in a physical search based on touch. However, some surfaces can be dangerous, uncomfortable, or even unsanitary to touch, such as a hot stove, or a garbage can. By guiding the user's hand via sound, a sonification system can precisely inform the location of a kettle handle or the top of a bin, without having to touch any surfaces to find it.

Is it safe to participate?

The survey itself is fully composed of multiple choice questions you can answer from the comfort of you own home, and it can be filled anonymously.

The in-person study you will be invited to participate at the end of the survey was also approved by RMIT's College Human Ethics Advisory Network.
